# Red Blood Cells (RBC)

## What it measures
The red blood cell (RBC) count measures the number of red blood cells in a volume of blood. Red blood cells carry oxygen throughout the body using hemoglobin.

## Why a value may be low
A low RBC count may be associated with anemia, blood loss, nutritional deficiencies, or other conditions. It is often interpreted together with hemoglobin and hematocrit rather than on its own.

## Why a value may be high
A high RBC count may be associated with dehydration, low blood-oxygen states, high altitude, or other factors. Only a clinician can interpret what a high count means for a particular person.

## Questions for your doctor
You could ask what might explain the result, whether related tests (such as hemoglobin or iron studies) are useful, and whether the value should be monitored.
