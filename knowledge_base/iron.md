# Iron

## What it measures
Iron studies (such as serum iron and ferritin) describe the amount of iron in the blood and the body's iron stores. Iron is needed to make hemoglobin and carry oxygen.

## Why a value may be low
A low iron or ferritin level may be associated with low dietary intake, reduced absorption, blood loss, or increased needs. Low iron is often considered alongside hemoglobin to assess for iron-deficiency patterns, which a clinician interprets.

## Why a value may be high
A high iron or ferritin level may be associated with inflammation, frequent transfusions, certain inherited conditions, or other factors. A single value does not establish a cause.

## Questions for your doctor
Iron is found in foods such as red meat, beans, and leafy greens (general nutrition information). You could ask what might explain the result, whether related tests are useful, and whether it should be monitored.
