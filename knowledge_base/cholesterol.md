# Cholesterol and Lipids

## What it measures
A lipid panel measures fats in the blood, including total cholesterol, LDL ("bad") cholesterol, HDL ("good") cholesterol, and triglycerides. These are used together to describe cardiovascular risk patterns.

## Why a value may be low
A low value depends on which lipid is measured. For example, a low HDL may be associated with diet, inactivity, or other factors, while very low total cholesterol is uncommon and interpreted by a clinician.

## Why a value may be high
Higher total cholesterol, LDL, or triglycerides may be associated with diet, activity level, genetics, weight, or other factors. A single result does not by itself determine cardiovascular risk, which a clinician assesses overall.

## Questions for your doctor
You could ask how the individual lipids fit together, whether lifestyle factors are relevant, and whether and when the panel should be repeated.
