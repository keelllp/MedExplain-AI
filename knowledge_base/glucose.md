# Glucose

## What it measures
Blood glucose is the amount of sugar in the blood, the body's main energy source. Fasting glucose and HbA1c (a longer-term average) are common ways to assess glucose.

## Why a value may be low
A low glucose level (hypoglycemia) may be associated with not eating, certain medications, or other conditions. Low values are interpreted in the context of symptoms and timing by a clinician.

## Why a value may be high
A higher-than-usual glucose level may be associated with diet, stress, certain medications, or conditions that affect how the body handles sugar. A single elevated reading does not by itself establish a diagnosis.

## Questions for your doctor
Useful questions include whether the test was fasting, whether follow-up tests (such as HbA1c) are appropriate, and whether the value should be re-checked.
