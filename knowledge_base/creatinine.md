# Creatinine

## What it measures
Creatinine is a waste product made by muscles and removed from the blood by the kidneys. Its level is often used as one general indicator of how the kidneys are filtering.

## Why a value may be low
A lower-than-usual creatinine level may be associated with lower muscle mass or other factors. A single low value is interpreted by a clinician in context.

## Why a value may be high
A higher-than-usual creatinine level may be associated with reduced kidney filtering, dehydration, higher muscle mass, certain medications, or other factors. A single value does not by itself establish a cause.

## Questions for your doctor
You could ask what might explain the result, whether related tests (such as eGFR or a repeat measurement) are useful, and whether it should be monitored.
