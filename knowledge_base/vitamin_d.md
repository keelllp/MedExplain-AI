# Vitamin D

## What it measures
The vitamin D test (usually 25-hydroxyvitamin D) reflects the body's vitamin D status. Vitamin D helps the body absorb calcium and supports bone health.

## Why a value may be low
A low vitamin D level may be associated with limited sun exposure, low dietary intake, certain conditions affecting absorption, or other factors. Low levels are common and are interpreted by a clinician.

## Why a value may be high
A high vitamin D level is less common and may be associated with high-dose supplementation. The meaning of a high value is assessed by a clinician in context.

## Questions for your doctor
General nutrition information (vitamin D is found in foods such as fatty fish and fortified milk, and the body also makes it from sunlight) may help your conversation. You could ask whether the level should be re-checked and what factors might explain it.
