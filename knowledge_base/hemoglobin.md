# Hemoglobin

## What it measures
Hemoglobin is the iron-containing protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body. The hemoglobin level reflects how much of this oxygen-carrying protein is present in the blood.

## Why a value may be low
A low hemoglobin level is often described as anemia. It may be associated with iron deficiency, blood loss, certain vitamin deficiencies, or other conditions. Low levels can sometimes be linked to tiredness or shortness of breath, but the cause can only be determined by a clinician.

## Why a value may be high
A higher-than-usual hemoglobin level may be associated with dehydration, living at high altitude, smoking, or other factors. The reason for a high value cannot be determined from a single result alone.

## Questions for your doctor
Useful questions can include what might be causing the result, whether any follow-up testing is appropriate, and whether the value should be re-checked over time.
