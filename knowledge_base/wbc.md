# White Blood Cells (WBC)

## What it measures
The white blood cell (WBC) count measures the number of white blood cells in the blood. White blood cells are part of the immune system and help the body respond to infection and inflammation.

## Why a value may be low
A low WBC count may be associated with certain infections, some medications, or other conditions. The significance depends on which types of white cells are affected, which a clinician can assess.

## Why a value may be high
A high WBC count may be associated with infection, inflammation, physical stress, or other factors. A single elevated value does not identify a specific cause.

## Questions for your doctor
Helpful questions include what might be causing the result, whether a differential (breakdown by cell type) is useful, and whether repeat testing is appropriate.
