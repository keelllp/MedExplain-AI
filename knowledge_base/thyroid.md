# Thyroid Markers (TSH, Free T4, Free T3)

## What it measures
Thyroid tests describe how the thyroid gland is functioning. TSH (thyroid-stimulating hormone) signals the thyroid; Free T4 and Free T3 are thyroid hormones that influence metabolism. They are usually interpreted together.

## Why a value may be low
A low TSH may be associated with an overactive thyroid pattern, while low Free T4/Free T3 may be associated with an underactive pattern, among other causes. Thyroid results are interpreted together by a clinician, not from one value alone.

## Why a value may be high
A high TSH may be associated with an underactive thyroid pattern, while high Free T4/Free T3 may be associated with an overactive pattern. The combination of values matters, and only a clinician can interpret them.

## Questions for your doctor
You could ask how the thyroid values fit together, whether additional tests (such as antibodies) are appropriate, and whether and when to repeat the panel.
